1-4: Real Madrid fight back against Girona to go top

Real Madrid have made a strong start to the 2018/19 LaLiga campaign. Following on from last week's home win over Getafe, the Whites secured a convincing 4-1 triumph over Girona in their first away game of the season. The Madrid goals came from Karim Benzema (twice), Sergio Ramos and Bale as the madridistas came from behind after Borja García had put the hosts in front. With two wins to their name, six goals for and one against, Julen Lopetegui's men sit top of the pile ahead of the final two games of the matchday.

Modric celebrates six years at Real Madrid

It has been six years since Luka Modric checked in at Real Madrid. Since his arrival on 27 August 2012, the Croatian schemer has been an instrumental figure in one of the most successful periods in the club's history. His impressive trophy haul speaks volumes of the role he's played: he has claimed no fewer than 14 trophies as a madridista (four Champions League crowns, three Club World Cups, three UEFA Super Cups, a LaLiga title, one Copa del Rey and two Spanish Super Cups). Modric made his bow two days after his unveiling and quite some debut it was, as Madrid overcome Barcelona to secure the Spanish Super Cup.The Golden Ball honour at the Russia World Cup.

Lopetegui turns 52

Julen Lopetegui today celebrates his 52nd birthday. The tactician was born on 28 August 1966 in Asteausu (Guipúzcoa) and, after having taken over the reins at Real Madrid in June, is embarking on his first campaign at the helm. "We'll be trying to give it our best and will fight to perform to the level that this club demands of us or even exceed it", stated Lopetegui when he was unveiled as the club's manager. Lopetegui has got his reign off to a fine start in LaLiga after winning the first two games, with six goals for and one against. He was previously the head coach of the 20-game unbeaten Spanish national team.

Asensio's decisive performance against Girona

Marco Asensio once again showcased his immense quality in the side's 4-1 away rout of Girona. The Spanish international was a constant menace and earned the two penalties Madrid were awarded in the game, the first of which was converted by Sergio Ramos with a Panenka-style spot-kick to level the scores at 1-1, before Karim Benzema slotted home the second to hand the Whites a 2-1 lead.
